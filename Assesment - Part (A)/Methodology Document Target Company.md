### Methodology Document: Target Company Research (Federer Profile)



**Project:** Business Analytics Internship – DeepThought



**Focus:** Hyderabad Hub | Basket A: Specialty Manufacturing



**Date:** May 11, 2026



###### 1\. Executive Summary of Research Process

The goal of this research was to identify "Federer" companies—technical manufacturing MSMEs that are promoter-led, R\&D-heavy, and showing active growth signals. The process moved from a Broad Universe of \~200 firms down to a Qualified List of 25, applying a strict 30% yield logic.



Every company on the final list is verified to have an in-house R\&D unit recognized by the DSIR (Department of Scientific \& Industrial Research), ensuring the "Differentiated" criteria is grounded in government-verified fact rather than marketing claims.



###### 2\. Sourcing Strategy (The Funnel)

**Step 1: Industrial Cluster Mapping (Geographic Initial Universe)**

Instead of a generic search, I mapped the industrial geography of Hyderabad to identify technical hubs. This created the "Initial Universe."



* **Genome Valley (Shameerpet/Turkapally):** Targeted for Specialty Biotech \& Diagnostics.



* **ALEAP Industrial Estate:** Targeted for women-led technical MSMEs.



* **IDA Pashamylaram \& Jeedimetla:** Targeted for Custom Synthesis \& Specialty Chemicals.



**Step 2: Database Mining (Primary Sources)**

* **DSIR Directory (July 2024):** Used as the primary filter for Criterion 3 (Differentiated). I filtered for companies in the "Andhra Pradesh/Telangana" region under Chemical and Biotech sectors.



* **TSIIC (Telangana State Industrial Infrastructure Corp):** Used the industrial park directories to verify the physical existence of manufacturing plants.



* **Bulk Drug Manufacturers Association (BDMA):** Cross-referenced member lists to find mid-sized promoter-led firms (excluding giants like Aurobindo).



**Step 3: Unconventional Intelligence (The "Extra Mile")**

To find "Growth Signals," I looked for evidence that isn't on a standard landing page:



* **Expo Attendee Lists:** Scanned the exhibitor lists of India Pharma Expo 2026 and BioAsia to find companies actively investing in market presence.



* **Naukri/LinkedIn Hiring Trends:** Searched for companies hiring for niche roles like "Downstream Scientist," "Analytical Method Validation," or "Plant Manager." High-volume technical hiring was treated as a "Strong" growth signal.





###### 3\. Verification \& Scoring Logic (The 6-Criteria Filter)

Criterion,Verification Source,"The ""Federer"" Test"

C1: Manufacturer,Google Maps \& Zauba Corp,"Verified the presence of a ""Factory"" or ""Plant"" address. If the site only showed a corporate office in a commercial building, they were disqualified."

C2: India-based,MCA Filings,Ensured the majority of directors are Indian residents and the company isn't a 100% subsidiary of a global multinational.

C3: Differentiated,DSIR Directory / Google Patents,Looked for a valid DSIR R\&D recognition number. Used Google Patents to check for Indian patent filings in the last 5 years.

C4: Technical DM,"LinkedIn / ""About Us""","Verified the Founder/MD has a PhD, M.Tech, or significant scientific pedigree (e.g., Ex-Scientist at IICT or CSIR)."

C5: Sector Tailwind,Invest India / PIB,"Linked the company’s product to current tailwinds (e.g., PLI Scheme for Bulk Drugs, China+1 for Specialty Chemicals)."

C6: Growth Signals,Tofler / News,"Checked for revenue growth in recent filings or announcements of new facility setups (e.g., in the new Sultanpur Medical Device Park)."



###### 4\. Segment Insights (What I Learned)

During this research, three key patterns emerged about the Hyderabad Specialty Manufacturing segment:



1. **The "Genome Valley" Premium:** Companies located in Genome Valley (e.g., Yapan Bio, Clonz Biotech) tend to have a much higher "Federer Score" because the infrastructure requires them to be high-tech. They are almost never "traders" or "commodity" players.



2\. **The "China+1" Transition:** Many mid-sized custom synthesis firms in Hyderabad (like Sapala Organics) are transitioning from "Cost-Arbitrage" to "Innovation-Led." They are no longer just making cheap chemicals; they are patenting the process of making those chemicals.



3\. **The Technical Founder Paradox:** I noticed that firms where the MD has a PhD often have a "stale" website but a very "active" DSIR recognition. This confirms the DeepThought hypothesis: these founders care about the science (product) but lack the systems (execution) to scale that science into a world-class organization.



###### 5\. Tools \& Code Used

* **Excel/Google Sheets:** For data structuring and Federer scoring.



* **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** For pinpointing technical founders and their educational history.



* **Zauba Corp / Tofler:** For financial "sanity checks" (Revenue < ₹500Cr).



* **Custom Prompting:** Used AI as a thinking partner to categorize complex chemical products (e.g., "Is Recombinant Human Serum Albumin a commodity or specialty?").



###### 6\. Quality Control (Fail List Methodology)

To ensure a high yield, I documented "Fails" as carefully as "Passes."



* **Fail Reason A:** PE-Owned (e.g., Avra Labs). Disqualified because decision-making is now institutional, not promoter-driven.



* **Fail Reason B:** Pure Service/CRO (e.g., Vimta Labs). Disqualified because they don't produce a physical product.



* **Fail Reason C:** Generic Pharma (e.g., many units in Jeedimetla). Disqualified because they compete on volume and price, not scientific differentiation.



**Conclusion:** The final list of 25 represents the top 10-15% of the Hyderabad MSME manufacturing ecosystem—companies that have the "Scientific Product" but need the "Scientific Execution" provided by DeepThought.
